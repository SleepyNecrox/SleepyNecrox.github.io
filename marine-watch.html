Project Description

Marine Watch: Diving for Diversity is a casual, educational underwater photography game designed to raise awareness about marine biodiversity and the impact of environmental degradation, specifically in the Philippine marine ecosystem. Players take on the role of Benjamin, a diver documenting ocean life using an in-game camera system, aiming to promote awareness about endangered species. The game integrates scientific and conservation-oriented learning with accessible gameplay mechanics, including species documentation, journal building, environmental cleanup, and resource management

This project is in partnership with Marine Wildlife Watch of the Philippines, a non-profit organization that aims to develop a better appreciation of the marine environment. The project serves as an advocacy-driven, nonprofit digital medium that blends entertainment with ecological education. A game of exploration and discovery meant to be enjoyed at a leisurely pace. The waters teeming with life underneath aims to acclimate the players with the idea that the underwater is something to be appreciated and not to be feared, while handling the delicate balance of respect and curiosity.

Design Process

We began with the goal of designing a peaceful, educational game that encourages discovery. Early iterations included time limits and scoring systems, but through playtesting, we removed pressure to emphasize curiosity over competition.
